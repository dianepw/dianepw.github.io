Diane Paces-Wiles, LCSW

As a psychotherapist, I work collaboratively with clients of all ages to clarify needs and bring lasting positive change. I have a passion for helping children and adolescents develop lifelong coping skills, supporting adults in overcoming challenges, and guiding families through significant life transitions.

My particular areas of interest include ADHD, high-functioning autism, anxiety, depression, and trauma. I also offer specialized resolution services for families in conflict and crisis, including reunification therapy, co-parent coaching, and court-ordered Parenting Coordination.

Building on a background in relational psychotherapy, I use cognitive-behavioral, play-based, mindfulness, and somatic techniques to inform our work together.

But, most importantly, I emphasize my clients’ personal strengths, encourage exploration, and build capacity to cope with challenges with compassion and humor.

Currently, I work as a therapist in Durham, NC. I welcome individuals from surrounding communities for in-person visits and throughout North Carolina via secure telehealth.

Offerings

Individual Therapy

I offer individual therapy for children (4 years and older), teenagers, and adults. I view therapy as a partnership (even with my youngest clients) and encourage ongoing feedback. My therapeutic approach is flexible and collaborative, drawing from different modalities based on client need. I have training in areas including play therapy, cognitive-behavioral therapy, sand tray therapy, mindfulness, and Somatic Experiencing.

Family Therapy

I work together with family members to resolve specific areas of conflict or improve overall communication and cooperation. Most often my family therapy sessions include all the members of the family, but can also focus on a parent-child or sibling relationship.

Parent Coaching

My parent coaching services focus on empowering parents to create environments of mutual respect and communication, understand behavioral concerns, and improve emotional management for both self and child. I am a certified Triple P (Positive Parenting Program) provider.

I also offer co-parent coaching to improve parent collaboration post-divorce.

Family Resolution

I offer the following Family Resolution services to navigate high-conflict divorce, ease communication, and repair relational issues.

  • Co-parent Coaching

    Your relationship may have ended, but your connection as co-parents continues. I offer co-parent coaching for support and skill-building to minimize reactivity, improve communication, and assist in decision-making so your children remain the focus.

  • Reunification Therapy

    I offer reunification therapy to address relationship disruptions between parent and child. Typically, reunification therapy is recommended or ordered by the courts when a child is resisting or refusing contact with a parent. Reunification therapy usually occurs in cases of high-conflict divorce, but may also be recommended in cases where a parent and child have had contact issues related to involvement with child protective services, military deployment, incarceration, or other causes of separation.

  • Parenting Coordination

    As a Parenting Coordinator, I may be appointed by the Court to help parents follow the provisions of their child custody order, reduce conflict, and negotiate decisions regarding their children.

About Somatic Experiencing

I have completed extensive training in Somatic Experiencing (SE), a body-oriented method for resolving trauma symptoms and relieving chronic stress. SE uses the nervous system’s own resources to gently restore stability, improve emotional regulation, and decrease overall distress. I enjoy educating both children and adults about the role of the nervous system in emotional and behavioral responses, and I incorporate embodied techniques into therapy when appropriate and desired.
